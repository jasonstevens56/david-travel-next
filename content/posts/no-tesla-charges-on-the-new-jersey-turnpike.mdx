<p>The New Jersey Turnpike Authority (JNTA) announced an exclusive EV charging deal for the New Jersey Turnpike service centers with <a href="applegreenelectric.com">Applegreen Electric</a>.  What this means is that the 64 Tesla Superchargers that are on the NJ Turnpike will be removed.  What that means is if you want to use a Tesla Supercharger, you will need to exit the NJ Turnpike and access these charges locally.  Of course, this will add time to your trip. </p>

<p>Applegreen will install chargers that will be compatible with all EV’s.  These new chargers will be initially installed by June 6<sup>th</sup> at the Vince Lombardi, Woodrow Wilson, Richard Stockton, James Fenimore Cooper, Joyce Kilmer, and Walt Whitman Service Areas.  With additional service centers coming online later this year.  Time will tell if their chargers are as quick as the Tesla Supercharger.  From a quick review, it seems that their rates are higher than Tesla’s.</p>

<p>Time will tell if this is a good deal for consumers or just a good deal for the JNTA.</p>

<p>Key Points:</p>

<p>New Deal: The NJTA has awarded an exclusive contract to Applegreen Electric for EV charging on the Turnpike.</p>

<p>Tesla Departure: Tesla will remove its Superchargers from Turnpike service areas.</p>

<p>Universal Chargers: Applegreen Electric's chargers will be compatible with all EVs.</p>

<p>New Tesla Charging: Tesla has built 116 new Supercharger stalls off the Turnpike.</p>

<p>Transition Timeline: Universal chargers are being rolled out at several service areas starting June 6th, with more locations coming online soon.</p>

<p>Location Transition:</p>

<p>June 6th: The chargers will be available at Vince Lombardi, Woodrow Wilson, Richard Stockton, James Fenimore Cooper, Joyce Kilmer, and Walt Whitman Service Areas.</p>

<p>July 2025: Molly Pitcher Service Area.</p>

<p>Fall 2025: Clara Barton and John Fenwick Service Areas.</p>
