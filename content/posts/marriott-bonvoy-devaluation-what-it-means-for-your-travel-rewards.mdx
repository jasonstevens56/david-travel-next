<p>It is amazing how much the value of the Bonvoy points have been compressed over time.  A quick look back from 2020, if you look at the highest rate for a category 8 hotel, shows a reduction in value of 40%.</p>

<figure class=wp-block-image size-full><img src=https://david.travel/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/marriott-marquis-houston-2a.jpg alt= class=wp-image-6243/></figure>

<p>Prior to these changes, <a href=https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/travel/how-much-are-my-marriott-bonvoy-points-worth#:~:text=How%20much%20are%2050%2C000%20Marriott,points%20are%20worth%20about%20%24720.>Nerd Wallet</a> calculated Bonvoy points as being worth $.09.  I wonder how that will change.  Nerd Wallet continues by saying that the Marriott Bonvoy points are below average for the hotel category.</p>

<p>With all this data, I do not know whether it makes sense to store up your Marriott Bonvoy points since they get compressed over time.  My suggestion is to use them as you accumulate them.</p>
