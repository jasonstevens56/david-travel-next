<p><a href="https://newsroom.aaa.com/2025/06/july4forecast/">American Automobile Association (AAA) released its annual press release</a> discussing the Independence Day holiday travel information.  Lots of good information in this press release. </p>

<p>First, 72.2 million people will travel at least 50 miles or more from home during the holiday week period (June 28th to July 6<sup>th</sup>). This represents an increase of 1.7 million from last year. </p>

<p>Travel By Car:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>61.6 million people will travel by car (a 2.2% increase over last year)</li>

<li>Last July 4th week, AAA responded to nearly 700,000 emergency roadside assistance calls</li>

<li>Summer gas prices are still the lowest they’ve been since 2021.</li>

<li>Hertz says Thursday, July 3rd is expected to be the busiest day to pick up vehicles</li>

<li>Most popular places to visit: Orlando, Denver, Boston, Oahu, and Seattle.</li>
</ul>

<p>Air Travel:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>5.84 million travelers will fly to their destinations (a 1.4% increase over last year</li>

<li>Travelers will pay on average $810 (a 4% increase from last year)</li>
</ul>

<p>By Other Modes:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Travel by other modes is expected to grow by 7.4% over last year. AAA projects 4.78 million people will travel by bus, train, or cruise.</li>
</ul>

<p>Best/Worst Times to Drive and Peak Congestion by Metro</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Wednesday, July 2 and Sunday, July 6 to be the busiest days on the road.</li>

<li>Afternoon hours will be the most congested.</li>

<li>Drivers should hit the road in the morning to avoid the worst traffic.</li>
</ul>
