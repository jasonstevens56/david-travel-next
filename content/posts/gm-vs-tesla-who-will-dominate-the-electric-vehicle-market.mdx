<p>General Motors has been making a major push in the EV Market.  They had tremendous growth in 2024 but can they maintain it to become a real competitor / threat to Tesla.  One of the key factors to look at is the profitability of GM’s EV division compared to Tesla?  From a <a href=https://stockdividendscreener.com/auto-manufacturers/gm-vs-tesla-which-stock-is-a-better-buy/>recent analysis</a>, Tesla had a gross profit per vehicle sold of roughly $7,700 in 2023 while GM had a comparable profit of $4,300 for all of its vehicles.  As to its EV, they have just cleared profitability on variable costs (not a pure profitability but just the margin cost to produce another EV). </p>

<figure class=wp-block-image size-full><img src=https://david.travel/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/image-2.jpeg alt= class=wp-image-6307/></figure>

<p><a href=https://stockdividendscreener.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/gm-vs-tesla-in-profit-per-vehicle.jpg></a></p>

<p>It looks like GM has a long way to go to match Tesla financially or even the financial results of their internal combustion engine vehicles.  But if they maintain their focus, they can make a profitable business of their EV initiative. </p>

<p>But for a long-term outlook can GM compete.  It will come down to which EV’s consumers want to buy.  Will they stay with Tesla current line up that includes the Model 3, Y, S and X as updated from time to time?  Or will they migrate to the Chevy’s and Cadillacs that GM is starting to produce?</p>

<p>Time will tell of course.  My take is that GM will slowly but surely take market share away from Tesla for a <a href=https://david.travel/has-tesla-jumped-the-shark-an-analysis-of-the-brand/>few reasons</a>.  First, Tesla’s vehicles are aging and people will want new and different models.  Second, the politics of Elon Musk will negatively impact the desirability of purchasing a Tesla.  A whole bunch of people who buy EV’s tend to be democrats and it is not a great sales strategy to piss them off.</p>

<p>I have a positive believe in the future of GM’s EV initiative. </p>
