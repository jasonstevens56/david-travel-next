<p>I found this interesting graphic showing the top paddles used ny Pickleball Pros</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="https://david.travel/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/516866700_18011185871773050_7338402521255626046_n.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-6830"/></figure>

<p>According to this graphic, which I know is annoying since there are only 2 colors with a bunch of shading, Joola represents roughly a third of the pro market place. Are their paddles so much better than Proton, Selkirk or Paddletex?</p>

<p>After speaking with several pickleball shop owners, the consensus is that Joola is paying more sponsorship money than the other manufacturers. My immediate reaction is that this seems to be a negative or jealous reaction from these shop owners.</p>

<p>As we dig in, we recognize that each player has their own individual needs and should choose their paddle accordingly. We recognize that money will influence their decisions. I have been unable to nail down the specific sponsorship money but based upon who they are sponsoring, I would concur that they are spending more money than other manufacturers.</p>

<p>Also, I would not that Joola has a considerable number of banned paddles with a breakout including:</p>

<p>JOOLA: Perseus 14mm MOD TA-15, Perseus 16mm MOD TA-15.</p>

<p>Gearbox: Pro Power Elongated.</p>

<p>ProKennex: Black Ace Ovation, Black Ace Pro, Black Ace LG, Black Ace XF</p>

<p>I acknowledge that Joola has the best pickleball paddle for the pros but I am not a pro!  Also, most players are not even close to be a pro.  As a result, we should not always choose a paddle because someone who is infinitely better than we are chose that paddle.</p>

<p>For example, people at my level may want more control while others may want more power.  We recommend that you choose a paddle that fits into your game and skill level.</p>
