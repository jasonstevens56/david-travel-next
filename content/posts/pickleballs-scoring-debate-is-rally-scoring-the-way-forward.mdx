<p>A <a href="https://usapickleball.org/news/usa-pickleball-releases-2025-pickleball-rulebook-featuring-new-updates-to-rules-of-play/">2025 Pickleball Rule Change</a> now endorses Rally Scoring in Doubles.  In <a href="https://usapickleball.org/pickleball-basics/scoring-position/">rally scoring pickleball</a>, a point is awarded to a team for every rally they win, regardless of which team is serving. This means that a team can score even when they are receiving the serve. Games are typically played to 21 points, and a team must win by two points.</p>

<p>Is this the future of Pickleball?  How many times have you played a game and you were stuck on a 2-2 score with neither side being able to break the tie.  Rally Scoring will speed up games and add a different level of excitement to pickleball. </p>

<p>Here's a more detailed breakdown:</p>

<p>Key Differences from Traditional Scoring:</p>

<p>Points on Every Rally:</p>

<p>In traditional pickleball, points are only scored when the serving team wins a rally. Rally scoring eliminates this restriction, allowing either team to score.</p>

<p>Simplified Serving:</p>

<p>In rally scoring, each team only has one server per side-out. The server switches sides after each point won during their serve.</p>

<p>Faster Pace:</p>

<p>Because points are scored more frequently, games in rally scoring tend to move at a quicker pace.</p>

<p>How it Works:</p>

<p>1. Serve:.</p>

<p>The game starts with one player serving from the right side of the court, and the serve must bounce once on the opponent's side.</p>

<p>2. Scoring:.</p>

<p>If the serving team wins the rally, they score a point and continue serving, alternating service positions. If the receiving team wins the rally, they score a point and become the new serving team.</p>

<p>3. Game Point:.</p>

<p>Games are played to 21 points, and a team must win by two points.</p>

<p>4. Side Change:.</p>

<p>Players change sides of the court after every point during their service turn.</p>

<p>Example:</p>

<p>Imagine a game starting at 0-0.</p>

<p>If the serving team wins the first rally, the score becomes 1-0, and they switch sides.</p>

<p>If the serving team wins the second rally, the score becomes 2-0, and they switch sides.</p>

<p>If the receiving team wins the third rally, the score becomes 1-2, and the receiving team becomes the new serving team.</p>

<p>The key question is will people adopt the rally scoring approach or keep playing the serve score system?  What do you think?</p>
