<p>The healthcare industry is facing a critical shortage of skilled professionals, and it's only projected to get worse. The Association of American Medical Colleges (AAMC) projects a national shortage of up to 124,000 physicians by 2033, including primary care physicians and specialists. In Florida alone, the shortage of primary care doctors could reach nearly 6,000. The U.S. could also be short 200,000 to 450,000 registered nurses (RNs) by 2025.</p>

<p>These shortages are driven by several factors, including burnout, an aging workforce, and the lengthy education and training process for healthcare professionals.</p>
