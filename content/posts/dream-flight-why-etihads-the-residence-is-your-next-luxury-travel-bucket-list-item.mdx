<p>As I compile my <a href="https://david.travel/the-thrill-of-the-fall-a-skydiving-bucket-list-addition/">bucket list of things to do</a>, some travel ideas came up including flying in the most luxurious manner.  Private jets are great but they are not typically the best option for trips to Asia, the Middle East or other travels more than 10 hours.  Typically, you need to take a commercial flight and see how good their first class is. </p>

<p>There have a been a few game changing inventions that have really impacted first class travel.  It started with lie down seats which allowed you to go from an airline seat to some form of bed.  Following this, when airlines introduces doors to first class, this allowed you some private place in a very public means of transportation.  As airlines have continued to add comforts in the search for the premium customers.</p>

<p>The latest development in this is <a href="https://www.etihad.com/en/plan/fly-with-etihad/our-cabins/the-residence">Etihad's The Residence</a>.  They have combined the luxury items of first class and then improved upon them.  The Residence is a luxurious, three-room private suite on the upper deck of their Airbus A380 aircraft.  It features a living room, a bedroom with a double bed, and a private bathroom with a shower. Of course they also include gourmet dining, and priority ground services.</p>

<p>If you want to book the Residence, it is only available on Etihad's Airbus A380 aircraft, primarily on routes connecting Abu Dhabi with London, Paris, New York, and Singapore.</p>

<p>You is only bookable as an upgrade option for First Class passengers.  Costs for the Residence will be in the range of $20,000-$30,000 one-way.</p>

<p>Is It Worth It?</p>

<p>For those seeking the pinnacle of commercial aviation luxury, The Residence offers an unparalleled experience. It's more than just a comfortable flight; it's a statement, a private sanctuary, and an unforgettable journey that truly redefines what it means to fly in style. No where else can you have your own private bathroom with its own shower.  The features in the Residence definitely add up to be placed on my bucket list. </p>
