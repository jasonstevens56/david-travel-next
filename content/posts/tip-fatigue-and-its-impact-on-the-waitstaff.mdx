<p><a href="https://david.travel/tipping-etiquette-in-united-states-a-complete-guide-for-travelers-2/">Tipping</a> has been changing since COVID.  During COVID and thereafter, people have been requesting tips for services that have normally not received tips.  Requesting tips became easier over time as more and more people pay their bills with credit cards and the terminals can add a request for a tip. </p>

<p>These additional tipping personnel now include retail staff, self-checkout, <a href="https://david.travel/tipping-is-not-required-at-free-hotel-breakfasts/">staff serving free meals</a> and grocery store workers.  As a result, consumers have developed tip fatigue.</p>

<p><strong>What is Tip Fatigue</strong></p>

<p>Tip fatigue refers to the growing reluctance among customers to tip, often due to the expanding number of situations in which tipping is requested. From counter service to takeout orders, customers are feeling overwhelmed by the constant pressure to add a gratuity.  This reluctance to tip ends up including all those who were request tips.</p>

<p><strong>How Tip Fatigue Affects Waiters</strong></p>

<p>Waiters, who rely on tips as a significant portion of their income, are particularly vulnerable to the effects of tip fatigue. Here are some of the ways it can impact them:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Decreased Earnings:</strong> As customers become more hesitant to tip or reduce the amount they tip, waiters' earnings can decline significantly.</li>

<li><strong>Lower Morale and Job Satisfaction:</strong> When tips decrease, waiters may feel undervalued and unappreciated. This can lead to lower morale, decreased job satisfaction, and higher turnover rates in the restaurant industry.</li>

<li><strong>Change in Customer Interactions:</strong> Tip fatigue can create awkward or tense interactions between customers and waiters. Waiters may feel pressure to provide exceptional service to ensure a good tip, while customers may feel burdened by the expectation to tip generously.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>What Could Happen</strong></p>

<p>If tip fatigue continues to grow, it could lead to a significant shift in the tipping culture in America. The current model in which the waitstaff rely on gratuities will become broken.  The waitstaff will need to make up for this loss of income somehow. </p>

<p>Some establishments may choose to institute a <a href="https://localgrubber.com/p/subway-institutes-mandatory-tip">mandatory tip</a> for its personnel to ensure appropriate compensation.</p>

<p>Other restaurants may choose to eliminate tipping altogether and switch to a service charge or higher menu prices, which could change the way waiters are compensated.</p>

<p>Tip fatigue is a complex issue with significant implications for waiters. By acknowledging the challenges they face and taking steps to address the problem, the restaurant industry can help ensure that waiters are fairly compensated and valued for their work.  Absent any changes, the hospitality industry and its clientele are going to see some difficult times ahead.</p>
