<p>From the <a href="https://corporate.airfrance.com/en/press-releases/air-france-restaurant-stops-rooftop-galeries-lafayette">hard to believe front</a>, an airline is opening a restaurant so that people can eat airline food without having to book a flight.  <a href="https://wwws.airfrance.us/">Air France</a> is opening a pop-up restaurants on the Rooftop at Galeries Lafayette in Paris.  This restaurant will be open from June 25<sup>th</sup> through August 20<sup>th</sup>.  <a href="https://bookings.zenchef.com/results?rid=376715&pid=1001&lang=en">Reservations</a> are required.  It will have open for lunch only with a price of 93 Euros including drinks. </p>

<p>The menu will be styled after their long-haul Business menus.  They are going with the them just like on board.  Lunch will include an appetizer, starter, main course, a selection of cheeses and dessert with tableware created for Air France in a setting similar to that on board the plane, incorporating the airline's Business meal tray.</p>

<p>The décor for this restaurant incorporates the airline’s color scheme with navy blue, white and red.  The rooftop will offer some incredible panoramic views of Paris.</p>

<p>I love the concept that Air France pushing a narrative that it serves high end restaurant food created by a Michelin three-star chef. Following this logic, Air France is trying to differentiate themselves from other airlines based upon the quality of their food offering.  Why travel on <a href="https://www.delta.com/">Delta</a> with their bland food when you can enjoy the food prepared by a Michelin chef?</p>

<p>Just imagine if Delta opened a restaurant serving their airline food. For domestic passengers, I guess that would include pretzels, biscuits and sun chips.  For long haul trips, I guess you could buy a box.  Actually, a restaurant has opened that serves Delta food, it is called <a href="https://shakeshack.com/">Shake Shack</a>.  For those of you who are unaware, Delta has <a href="https://news.delta.com/prepare-takeoff-delta-and-shake-shack-expand-onboard-partnership">celebrated its partnership</a> with Shake Shack by announcing that they would be serving burgers prepared by Shake Shack for first class passengers.  If you travel on Delta, you can get a burger but with Air France, you can choose between a fish like grouper, shrimp or a chicken dish.  Personally, if you can not get a milk shake and French fries with my burger, why order it?</p>
