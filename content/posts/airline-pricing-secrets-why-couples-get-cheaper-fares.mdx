<p>Our Friends at the <a href="https://thriftytraveler.com/news/airlines/delta-pricing-quirk/">Thrifty Traveler</a> found a quirky pricing structure built into the reservation system at Delta, American Airlines and United.  The fare for one passenger is significantly higher than the price per person if you add a second traveler to the same itinerary. This is not a glitch or an error.  These airlines have intentional set up the fares this way.</p>

<p>If you want to book the lowest priced tickets, you need to travel with someone.  For those of us  who do not have friends or a loved one, this unfair.  But as they say, life is unfair.</p>

<p>Here is an example. A one-way Delta flight from Minneapolis-St. Paul (MSP) to Miami (MIA) in early September might show a price of $199 for a single passenger. But, if you adjust your search to include two passengers, the per-person fare astonishingly drops to just $118. That's a difference of $81 per ticket.</p>

<p>This begs the question on why are airlines pricing their seats this way.  It is all about the airlines trying to maximize their profits.  They recognize that each passenger has a different price point for their flights.  Families and couples traveling for pleasure may be more price sensitive than a person traveling for business.  Typically, the business person would be looking for 1 seat and the family would be looking for 2 or more seat.  As a way to segment the customers, airlines try to structure the fares to identify who is more price sensitive than the others.  As a result, individual seats will be seen as business travel and less price sensitive.  They therefore see the higher price.</p>

<p>In the past, airlines would use Saturday night stay as a way to identify business vs pleasure travels.  But competition from low-cost airlines have forced the other airlines to do away with this requirement. </p>

<p>The lesson to be learned is if you are traveling with anyone, you need to check pricing for multiple people when researching because it may be cheaper.  Airlines have a bunch of ways to increase the price that they will charge you.  My suggestion is to check multiple options when booking</p>
