<p>I’m a huge fan of Apple AirTags. Having the peace of mind that you can track a lost bag anywhere in the world is a game-changer for all kind of travelers. While an AirTag won’t stop an airline from misplacing your luggage, it is often the only thing that <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/news/airtag-battery/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">expedites its recovery</a> when things go wrong.</p>

<p>However, there is one major flaw in the set it and forget it strategy: the battery.</p>

<p></p>

<p><strong>The Silent Battery Problem</strong></p>

<p>AirTags are incredibly low-maintenance. You slip one into your suitcase or wallet and usually don't think about it again for months. But unlike your iPhone, which demands a daily charge, an AirTag battery typically lasts about a year.</p>

<p>Because they don't require daily attention, it is incredibly easy to forget they need power at all. While Apple does send low-battery notifications through the Find <a href="https://david.travel/apples-new-airtag-feature-never-lose-your-belongings-again-airlines-will-have-access-to-the-find-my-feature/">My app</a>, these alerts often arrive at the worst possible times—like when you’re already at the airport or in the middle of a terminal with no spare batteries in sight.</p>

<p></p>

<p><strong>How to Proactively Check Your AirTag Battery</strong></p>

<p>Don’t wait for a notification. It takes less than 30 seconds to check your status before you start packing:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Open the Find My app on your iPhone.</li>

<li>Tap the Items tab at the bottom.</li>

<li>Select the specific AirTag you want to check.</li>

<li>Look for the Warning: Apple no longer shows a specific percentage. If the battery is healthy, you won't see an icon. If it's nearing the end, a red <a href="https://support.apple.com/en-us/102600" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">"Low Battery"</a> banner will appear under the item's name.</li>
</ul>

<p></p>

<p><strong>Pro-Tip: The Bitter Coating Trap</strong></p>

<p>When you do replace the battery, you'll need a standard CR2032 lithium 3V coin battery.</p>

<p>Warning: Many brands (like Duracell) apply a bitter coating to these batteries to prevent children from swallowing them. This coating can actually <a href="https://support.apple.com/en-lamr/102600" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">prevent the battery from making a connection</a> with the AirTag. If you buy these, you may need to wipe the battery down with an alcohol swab before it will work.</p>

<p>Add it to Your Packing Checklist</p>

<p>Most travelers obsess over passports and boarding passes but forget the small devices protecting their expensive gear. An AirTag is only a tracking tool if it’s powered on.</p>

<p>Before your next trip, make Check AirTag batteries a key part of your pre-travel routine. Replacement batteries are inexpensive and take less than a minute to <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jylbd-SF28k" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">swap out</a>.</p>

<p>Because the worst time to discover a dead battery is when your luggage is already missing.</p>
